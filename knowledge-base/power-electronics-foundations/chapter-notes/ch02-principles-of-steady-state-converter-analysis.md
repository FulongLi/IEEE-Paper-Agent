# Chapter 2: Principles of Steady-State Converter Analysis

## Retrieval Tags

steady-state analysis, volt-second balance, charge balance, duty ratio, CCM, ripple approximation

## Core Ideas

- Periodic steady state is characterized by repeated switching cycles with no net long-term drift in inductor current or capacitor voltage.
- Inductor volt-second balance and capacitor charge balance are the central tools for deriving ideal conversion ratios.
- The small-ripple approximation simplifies converter analysis by separating average quantities from switching ripple.

## Useful IEEE Language

- "The steady-state conversion ratio is obtained by applying volt-second balance to the inductor over one switching period."
- "Under the small-ripple approximation, the switching waveform can be represented by its cycle-averaged value for low-frequency analysis."
