# Chapter 11: Inductor Design

## Retrieval Tags

inductor sizing, current ripple, saturation current, copper loss, core loss, thermal design

## Core Ideas

- Inductor design translates ripple, current stress, inductance, loss, and size requirements into a physical magnetic component.
- A practical inductor must satisfy inductance tolerance, saturation margin, winding loss, core loss, thermal limits, and manufacturability.
- Converter performance is strongly affected by inductor ripple, series resistance, and high-frequency loss mechanisms.

## Useful IEEE Language

- "The inductor value is selected to meet the specified current-ripple constraint over the full operating range."
- "The magnetic design is verified against saturation current and temperature-rise requirements."
