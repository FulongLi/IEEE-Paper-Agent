# Chapter 20: Power and Harmonics in Nonsinusoidal Systems

## Retrieval Tags

harmonics, nonsinusoidal power, power factor, RMS, apparent power, distortion

## Core Ideas

- Nonsinusoidal voltage and current waveforms require harmonic-based interpretation of power, RMS values, and power factor.
- Distortion power and displacement effects should be distinguished when analyzing rectifiers and grid-connected converters.
- Harmonic content affects losses, electromagnetic compatibility, grid standards, and filter design.

## Useful IEEE Language

- "The input-current harmonic spectrum is used to evaluate distortion and compliance with power-quality requirements."
- "The power-factor degradation arises from both phase displacement and waveform distortion."
