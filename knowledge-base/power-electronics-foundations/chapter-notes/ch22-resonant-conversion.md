# Chapter 22: Resonant Conversion

## Retrieval Tags

resonant converter, tank circuit, LLC, series resonance, parallel resonance, frequency control

## Core Ideas

- Resonant converters use tank networks to shape voltage and current waveforms, often enabling reduced switching loss and high-frequency operation.
- Power transfer is controlled by switching frequency, tank impedance, load condition, and transformer characteristics.
- Resonant design requires attention to gain curves, circulating current, soft-switching range, and component stress.

## Useful IEEE Language

- "The resonant tank shapes the converter waveforms to support high-frequency operation with reduced switching loss."
- "The voltage-gain characteristic is evaluated over the required input-voltage and load range to ensure regulation."
