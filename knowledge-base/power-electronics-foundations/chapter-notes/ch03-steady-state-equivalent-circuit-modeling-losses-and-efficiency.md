# Chapter 3: Steady-State Equivalent Circuit Modeling, Losses, and Efficiency

## Retrieval Tags

equivalent circuit, nonideal components, conduction loss, switching loss, efficiency, loss model

## Core Ideas

- Real converters deviate from ideal conversion ratios because switches, diodes, inductors, capacitors, and interconnects introduce loss and voltage drops.
- Steady-state equivalent circuits help connect physical nonidealities to measurable efficiency and regulation behavior.
- Loss modeling supports design tradeoffs among semiconductor selection, magnetic design, thermal constraints, and operating point.

## Useful IEEE Language

- "The nonideal converter model includes parasitic resistances and device voltage drops to predict efficiency under load variation."
- "The measured efficiency profile reflects the relative contribution of conduction, switching, magnetic, and auxiliary losses."
