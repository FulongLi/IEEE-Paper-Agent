# Chapter 23: Soft Switching

## Retrieval Tags

soft switching, ZVS, ZCS, resonant transition, switching loss, snubber, high-frequency conversion

## Core Ideas

- Soft switching reduces switching loss by arranging voltage or current waveforms so that semiconductor transitions occur under favorable conditions.
- Zero-voltage switching and zero-current switching improve efficiency and enable higher switching frequency, but may require auxiliary networks or resonant transitions.
- Practical soft-switching design must account for load range, device capacitance, dead time, circulating energy, and control timing.

## Useful IEEE Language

- "The proposed switching sequence achieves zero-voltage switching over the specified load range."
- "Soft-switching operation reduces turn-on loss, but the associated circulating current must be considered in the efficiency analysis."
