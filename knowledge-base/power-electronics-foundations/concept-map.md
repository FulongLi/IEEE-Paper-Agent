# Power Electronics Concept Map

## Converter Families

- Non-isolated DC-DC converters: buck, boost, buck-boost, Cuk, SEPIC, Zeta.
- Isolated DC-DC converters: flyback, forward, push-pull, half-bridge, full-bridge, phase-shifted full-bridge, LLC resonant converter.
- DC-AC inverters: voltage-source inverter, current-source inverter, multilevel inverter, grid-tied inverter.
- AC-DC rectifiers: diode rectifiers, controlled rectifiers, power-factor-correction front ends.
- AC-AC converters: matrix converters, cycloconverters, solid-state transformers.

## Operating Principles

- Volt-second balance for inductors in periodic steady state.
- Charge balance for capacitors in periodic steady state.
- Continuous conduction mode and discontinuous conduction mode.
- Ripple approximation and small-ripple assumptions.
- Duty-ratio control and conversion-ratio derivation.
- Switching frequency, ripple, loss, size, and efficiency tradeoffs.

## Modeling and Control

- Averaged switch modeling.
- Small-signal perturbation and linearization.
- Control-to-output transfer function.
- Line-to-output transfer function.
- Output impedance and input impedance.
- Feedback compensation: proportional-integral control, type-II compensation, type-III compensation.
- Loop gain, crossover frequency, phase margin, gain margin, and transient response.

## Magnetics and Passive Components

- Inductor selection, saturation current, copper loss, and core loss.
- Transformer turns ratio, magnetizing inductance, leakage inductance, and isolation.
- Capacitor equivalent series resistance and ripple-current rating.
- Filter design for conducted electromagnetic interference.

## Semiconductor Devices and Losses

- MOSFET, IGBT, diode, synchronous rectifier, GaN HEMT, and SiC MOSFET.
- Conduction loss, switching loss, gate-drive loss, reverse-recovery loss, and dead-time effects.
- Safe operating area, thermal impedance, junction temperature, and heatsinking.

## Research-Oriented Themes

- High-efficiency converter design under wide input-voltage or load ranges.
- High-power-density design through higher switching frequency and advanced devices.
- Robust control under parameter uncertainty and load transients.
- Soft-switching and resonant techniques for loss reduction.
- EMI-aware converter layout and filter co-design.
- Digital control, model predictive control, and data-driven converter optimization.
